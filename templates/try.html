What is PM10?


PM10

stand for particulate matter with a diameter between 2.5 and 10 micrometers (μm).
Daily average limit:
50 µg/m3 for Europe and 150 µg/m3 for United State.

Prediction of PM10

The goal is to inform about air pullotion in a particular day and give some advice to improve our life-style and be eco-friendly.
PM10's concentration has impacts on climate and precipitations, as consequence they can even affect negatively human health.
When the particles are inhaled they remain in the respiratory tract depending on their size.
The smallest ones are even more damaging to the cardiovascular system and can also cause respiratory diseases.
So it's important to be-ecofriendly and be informed not only for our health but for our earth's health too.
Remember that only Milan's municipalities positions are valid.

Model:


The model is based on PM10's measurements on the area of study that took 3 years (25 May 2015 - 25 May 2018), associated with weather temperature, humidity and wind speed of the same period. Additional information about population's density of Milan's municipalities, air pollution detection sensors, weekday, month and season have been added to the model. Also the previous two days of weather temperature are added (if available).
The prediction is done in real time!
